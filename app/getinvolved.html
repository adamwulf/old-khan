The biggest thing is getting the word out.  The students who use the site seem to really get excited by how quickly and deeply they can learn from it.  Right now (11/2010), there are about 1,000,000 students using the site per month; no reason why it shouldn't be 20 million! You can help get the word out in your local community by joining our volunteer outreach group or visiting our Forge page for outreach. You can also organize a meetup in your city.

Other simple things you can do include answering questions in the Q&A section below videos you watch.  You can also report problems, including video mistakes, using the "Report a problem" link at the bottom of the page where you see the problem.

If you want to be more actively involved and you know a foreign language, translating the videos would be huge! Even better, if you know some topic deeply, are a good, down-to-earth teacher, AND know a foreign language, you could re-teach some subset of the videos!

If you're an expert (especially at something that Sal's unlikely to be able to learn in the next 5 years) and want to become a minor education celebrity (but a major one in your field), make a handful of videos and contribute them.  If they gel with the style--conversational while focused on intuition and rigor--of the current library, it might make sense for you to make a playlist or two (with all credit going to you of course).

If you like to write code, you can help with the Khan Academy open source web application.

And of course, you can always donate money. The Khan Academy is a not-for-profit 501(c)(3) organization. 

If you have an idea for how your organization can help make Khan Academy better, please tell us about it by sending an email to the publicly accessible Khan Academy Support Offers group.  Although we don't have time to reply to individual messages, we do read your messages whenever we get a chance.  Please be as clear and concise as possible about what you are proposing and what, if anything, you would expect in return (e.g. a link to your site, or access to anonymized data, financial compensation, etc.).  If you don't hear from us right away, it might mean that we don't see a good fit, or it might mean that we just don't have the time and resources to pursue working with you right away.  Either way, we sincerely appreciate you taking the time to contact us.  Please note that your messages will be publicly accessible, so don't post private or confidential information.

Help Educate the World!

Our mission is to provide a free, world-class education to anyone, anywhere. With that said, Sal only speaks English (and very barely passable Bengali). With your help, we might be able to reach the 5.6 billion people who speak other languages. Let's translate the Khan Academy into all of the world's languages!

If you feel that you are capable of dubbing a video or even re-doing videos in a non-English language, here's a chance to become a hero and minor celebrity to your people. Millions of people could be learning from you across the globe. More importantly, it is a tangible opportunity to leverage your skills and talents to seriously change the world in a very good way. For all we know, students 25 years from now may be learning negative numbers from YOU! This might even be a way to preserve languages.

Count me in! What should I do?

Do you enjoy teaching? Then you are probably as excited about this as we think you should be. If you can convey that energy and passion in your voice as you reach hundreds of millions of students we want to join forces with you. The best way to move forward would be if you were to dub or re-do the videos for the subject(s) you want to be considered for and upload them to someplace (like YouTube or Vimeo):

  • Mathematics:Solving Inequalities
  • Chemistry:Introduction to the Atom
  • Physics:Newton's Third Law of Motion
  • Biology: Introduction to Evolution and Natural Selection

Then email the link to translations@khanacademy.org. In your email, include a bit about your background (education, occupation, etc.) and your sense of how many videos you could do and how long you think it would take. If you are a good fit, we'll coordinate with you on how best to move forward!

If you have any questions about translations or would like to connect with and share any information that might help others, please visit the Khan Academy Forge page for translators and join the discussion group.

We look forward to educating the world with you!
